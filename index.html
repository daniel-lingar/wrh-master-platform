The WRH Pilot Program: "The Mechanism"

Our flagship offering is a 30-session, three-season psychoeducational program designed to help participants understand and interrupt maladaptive patterns stemming from trauma. The program is structured as a "serialized psychological experience," building curiosity and self-recognition through a case study approach, rather than direct personal disclosure.

Program Structure: Every Session

Anchor (5-10 min)

Regulate the room and bring participants into the present.

Episode (Case Study Segment)

A piece of a case study (e.g., "The Architect") told through behavior, not emotion.

Mechanism Breakdown

Translate the observed behavior into system logic.

Mirror Moment

Allow silent self-recognition among participants.

Tool / Shift

Provide a light tool for immediate control and change.

Cliffhanger Close

Open a loop to encourage continued engagement and processing.

Season Overview

Season 1: "THE SYSTEM" (Foundations)

Focuses on understanding the origin and reinforcement of maladaptive patterns (e.g., NICU wiring, childhood trauma, freeze response).

Season 2: "THE DRIVERS" (Deeper Mechanisms)

Explores the underlying psychological and biological mechanisms (e.g., nervous system memory, triggers, dissociation, attachment patterns).

Season 3: "THE INTERRUPT" (Control & Change)

Equips participants with real-time control strategies (e.g., Yellow Light/Red Light protocols, vetoing automatic responses, rewriting loops).

  • Emphasizes pattern recognition over personal disclosure.

  • Structured, psychoeducational intervention reduces liability and increases engagement.

Language Alignment
