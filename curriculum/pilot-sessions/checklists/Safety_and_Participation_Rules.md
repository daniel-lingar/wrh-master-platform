# Safety & Participation Rules

## Participant Version (Read in Session 00)

> **1. No Introductions. No Forced Sharing.**  
> In this program, we do **not** “go around the room.” You will not be asked to stand up, give your name, or explain why you are here.
> - You will never be required to talk about your trauma, charges, addiction, or family.  
> - You can pass on any question at any time.  
> - You can stay completely silent for all 30 sessions and still be participating appropriately.

> **2. This is Psychoeducation, Not Group Therapy.**  
> We study how nervous-system patterns, stress responses, freeze, shame, and survival adaptations work, using one documented case study as the teaching map. You are not here to perform your pain; you are here to learn the system.

> **3. Your Participation Has Limits and Choice.**  
> - If you feel overwhelmed or flooded, you may step out according to site procedure.  
> - Yellow/Red Light safety protocols are in place to keep the room paced and to route needs beyond WRH scope to approved support.  
> - You are not “in trouble” for needing space.

> **4. The “Plan B” for Overload and Focus Problems.**  
> If being in this room feels intense, or you can’t focus:  
> - Ground once: feet on the floor, one slow exhale.  
> - Drop performance: you are not here to impress anyone.  
> - Catch one sentence: just hold on to one idea that lands today.  
> - Write one word about it later. That is enough for this session.

> **5. The Only Rule About Other People’s Stories.**  
> - No crosstalk, no advice-giving, no “fixing” anyone.  
> - If someone else’s story affects you, that is information about your own system. WRH teaches what to do with activation without requiring disclosure.

---
*“If you never say a word, but you keep showing up and quietly keeping one sentence per session, you are still allowed to learn here.”*

## Institutional Summary Paragraph

**Safety & Participation Model:** WRH is a non-exposure, non-processing psychoeducational curriculum. Participants are never required to introduce themselves to the group or disclose personal histories. The program eliminates “go-around-the-room” introductions and forced sharing, and instead uses standardized safety boundaries, Yellow/Red Light procedures, micro-regulation scripts, and an opt-in participation model for participants who may experience hyperarousal, shutdown, concentration difficulty, or overload in group settings. Participants may remain silent while still engaging through structured noticing tasks and written reflections. Needs that exceed WRH’s non-clinical educational scope are routed through host-site procedures, approved support staff, crisis resources, or emergency services as appropriate.
