# Facilitator Readiness Checklist

## Session Delivery Readiness

This checklist is intended to support consistent room preparation and disciplined delivery before each WRH session. Facilitators may use it as a pre-session control document to confirm that the environment, materials, and safety procedures are in place before participants enter the room.

---

## Room Setup

- [ ] Room reserved and available for the full session window, including setup and closeout time.
- [ ] Seating arranged to support facilitator visibility, participant exits, and controlled movement.
- [ ] Entry and exit pathways clear and unobstructed.
- [ ] Whiteboard, markers, and presentation materials ready for use.
- [ ] Lighting, temperature, and noise conditions checked and adjusted as needed.
- [ ] Restroom access confirmed and explained to participants if required by site procedure.
- [ ] Water, tissues, and basic comfort supports available if permitted by site policy.

---

## Participant Materials

- [ ] Attendance sheet or roster prepared.
- [ ] Session notes or facilitator script printed or open for reference.
- [ ] Participant handouts available in the correct quantity.
- [ ] Plan B wallet card copies available when needed.
- [ ] Pens, pencils, or required writing materials available.
- [ ] Confidentiality or participation forms ready for Session 00 or new participants.
- [ ] Any site-required evaluation or assessment forms prepared.

---

## Safety Protocols

- [ ] Yellow Light and Red Light procedures reviewed before session start.
- [ ] Co-facilitator, support staff, or escalation contact identified.
- [ ] Site-specific emergency and crisis procedures confirmed.
- [ ] Private step-out space identified for participants who need a pause.
- [ ] Mandatory reporting and incident documentation requirements reviewed.
- [ ] Facilitator has reviewed the non-disclosure, non-pressure instructional boundary.
- [ ] Post-session follow-up plan identified for any participant who becomes activated.

---

## Facilitator Control Check

- [ ] Session objective reviewed and understood.
- [ ] Opening anchor and closing sequence prepared.
- [ ] Time control plan set for each segment.
- [ ] Tone aligned to the WRH instructional model: direct, structured, and operational.
- [ ] Personal distractions minimized so full attention remains on room control and participant pacing.

---

Completion of this checklist helps ensure that the room, the material, and the safety architecture are aligned before instruction begins.
