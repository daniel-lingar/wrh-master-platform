# FACILITATOR DECOMPRESSION PROTOCOL
## **Systems Maintenance for High-Intensity Delivery**
**Version:** 1.1 (April 2026)  
**Tone:** Calm, analytical, non-shaming, non-clinical, and systems-focused

---

## **1. THE RATIONALE**
Facilitating the WRH curriculum can create significant cognitive and emotional load. The facilitator is managing pacing, room regulation, safety boundaries, group attention, and their own nervous-system state while delivering high-impact material.

This protocol exists to reduce burnout risk, preserve delivery fidelity, and prevent facilitators from carrying session material home as unresolved stress.

---

## **2. IMMEDIATE POST-SESSION RESET (0–15 MIN)**
**Objective:** Exit facilitator mode and ground the system.

1.  **Physical Exit:** Leave the room after the last participant exits. Do not linger for informal processing unless there is a safety issue or host-site procedure requires follow-up.
2.  **Somatic Discharge:**
    *   **The Shake:** Shake out your hands and feet for 30 seconds to release physical tension.
    *   **The Exhale:** Complete three rounds of slow breathing, such as inhaling for 4 and exhaling for 8 through pursed lips.
3.  **Environment Shift:** Change your physical environment. Step outside, change rooms, drink water, or wash your face with cold water.

---

## **3. THE DATA DOWNLOAD (15–30 MIN)**
**Objective:** Move session observations from working memory into the record.

1.  **Fidelity Log:** Complete the session fidelity checklist. Note any Yellow/Red Light incidents.
2.  **Delivery Check:** Did you stay calm, analytical, non-shaming, and non-clinical? Did the room remain regulated? Record observations without judgment.
3.  **Warm-Handoff Review:** If a participant reached a Red Light or expressed safety concerns, ensure the handoff to host-site leadership, licensed staff, crisis resources, or emergency services was documented according to site procedure.

---

## **4. SYSTEMIC MAINTENANCE (24 HOURS)**
**Objective:** Prevent facilitator overload from accumulating.

1.  **The Firewall:** Do not take participant stories home as personal responsibility. You are delivering an educational framework, not becoming the container for each participant’s pain.
2.  **Physical Regulation:** Prioritize sleep, hydration, food, and physical movement within 24 hours of a session.
3.  **Peer Review:** If a session was particularly intense, schedule a brief systems check with another facilitator, supervisor, or approved site contact.

---

## **5. RED LIGHT INDICATORS FOR FACILITATORS**
If you notice the following, initiate a **Full System Reset** and notify the appropriate supervisor or site contact when needed:

- Becoming overly emotional, cynical, confrontational, or detached during delivery.
- Avoiding session preparation.
- Sleep disruption, irritability, or persistent activation after delivery days.
- Feeling personally responsible for participant outcomes beyond your role.
- Blurring WRH’s non-clinical educational scope with therapy, crisis intervention, or personal rescue.
