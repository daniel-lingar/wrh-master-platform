Reveal that `The Crash` doesn’t come from one big event — it comes from quiet, invisible stress accumulation that finally crosses the line.

## [10:00 – 30:00] THE HOOK — The Slow Build That No One Sees

### Purpose

Illustrate the insidious accumulation of stress that leads to `The Crash`.

### The What (Facilitator Script)

**The Accumulation**

"`The Architect` wakes up already carrying yesterday’s load. Small things. Nothing dramatic. `The Hum` is a little louder than usual. A text from probation. Traffic that feels too loud. A bill he can’t open. Another quiet night where `The Mask` has nothing to feed on."

"Each `activation` adds one more drop to the `ledger`. The body doesn’t reset. It just keeps score. Day after day the pressure builds. Not one explosion. Not one giant trauma. Just the slow, silent stacking of every `trigger` that hits the old wiring. The `Glass Box` alarms. The `blanket-night freeze`. `The Hum` that never got the all-clear."

"Until one random Tuesday — nothing special — `The System` hits the line. `The Threshold`. `The Crash` doesn’t announce itself. It just takes over. `The Disappearing Act` flips on. The brain goes underwater. `The Miss` is already in motion before he even realizes the gauge red-lined."

"From the outside it looks random. From the inside he knows: it wasn’t random. It was the quiet buildup finally crossing `The Threshold`."

"The body doesn’t need one big event to break. It only needs enough small ones to push it past the line it was wired to fear."

### The How (Facilitator Cues)

| Cue Type | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pacing** | Lean in. Voice intense, like you’re watching a pressure gauge climb in real time. |
| **Fidelity Check** | (Approx. 20:00) Are participants tracking the concept of `stress accumulation`? Is there any visible recognition of this pattern in their own lives? |
