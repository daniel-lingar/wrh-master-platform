## [10:00 – 30:00] THE HOOK — The Three Responses in Action

### Purpose

Illustrate the practical manifestation of `Fight`, `Flight`, and `Freeze` responses, highlighting `The Architect`'s default to `freeze`.

### The What (Facilitator Script)

**The Plays**

"`The Architect`’s nervous system only has three plays when `The Hum` spikes or `The Threshold` gets crossed."

"`Fight`: He locks in and goes full beast mode in the kitchen — tickets flying, chaos everywhere. He attacks the pressure head-on. Looks like discipline. Looks like leadership. But it’s `The Hum` wearing armor."

"`Flight`: The second the chaos ends he wants to run. Not literally sprint out the door — but escape into anything that drowns `The Hum`. Substances. Overwork. Anything to get away from the quiet."

"`Freeze`: This is the default. Envelope on the counter. Probation text. Quiet apartment. Court date coming. His body chooses the oldest code — disappear. Don’t move. Don’t engage. Don’t exist. The same `freeze` from the `Glass Box` and the `blanket night` at age six takes over completely."

"Most people cycle through all three. `The Architect` almost never fights or runs anymore. He defaults straight to `freeze`. Because that’s the only response the wiring ever proved would keep him alive. `Fight` got him hurt as a kid. `Flight` wasn’t possible in the incubator. `Freeze` worked every single time."
