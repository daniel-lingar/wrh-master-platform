**Purpose:** Re-establish safety and control. Read verbatim.

---

## **[0:10 – 0:30] THE HOOK / CORE CONCEPT**
**Purpose:** Introduce the WRH mechanism-first model through a documented case-study frame.

**Core Concept:**  
This is a psychoeducational curriculum. We study the mechanisms of C-PTSD, survival adaptation, shame cycles, freeze responses, and executive-function disruption through one documented life referred to here as the Architect. This is not a therapy group. We are learning the map.

The Architect was born three pounds, four ounces in 1982. He spent his first thirty days in a glass box. He had last rites at seven days old. By the time he was five, his home was a war zone. By the time he was forty, he had lost a brother, a marriage, custody of his children, and a decade to addiction. He is also still here — and he turned his nervous system into a curriculum.

---

## **[0:30 – 0:55] CASE STUDY DEEP DIVE**
**Purpose:** Map Daniel's pattern step-by-step without asking participants to disclose their own histories.

**Safety Frame:**  
Distribute the Plan B handout and review the confidentiality and participation guidelines. Walk through the Yellow/Red Light protocol. Follow host-site procedures for any required acknowledgement forms.

*"You are in control. You can pass, step out, or stay silent."*

**Facilitator Note:** Session 00 is where trust is built or damaged. Do not rush the safety frame. The next 29 sessions only work if participants understand that WRH is non-disclosure-based, non-clinical, and voluntary in engagement style.
