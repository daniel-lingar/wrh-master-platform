# WRH Pilot Outcomes & Proof-of-Concept
*Evaluation framework for non-clinical trauma psychoeducation in real-world settings.*

---

## **Purpose of This Document**
This document outlines the intended pilot measurement structure for the WRH Pilot Deployment. It is designed to help partner organizations evaluate implementation, participant engagement, retention, tool recall, facilitator usability, and program fit.

This is a pilot-stage outcomes framework. Final claims should be based on completed pilot data, participant feedback, facilitator observations, and partner review.

---

## **The Problem: Retention, Overload, and Real-World Follow-Through**
Many psychoeducational programs struggle when participants are overloaded, dysregulated, ashamed, withdrawn, or inconsistent in attendance. WRH is designed to improve engagement by using **spaced repetition**, **plain-language mechanisms**, **non-disclosure participation**, **active application**, and **low-arousal learning design**.

The goal is not to claim clinical treatment outcomes. The goal is to measure whether participants can remember, recognize, and apply simple pattern-interruption tools in real-world settings.

## **Pilot Site A: Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas**
*Status: Planned 2026 pilot launch*

### **Target Metrics**
The following key performance indicators (KPIs) are proposed for the Stepping Stones cohort:

| Metric | Target | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cohort Retention** | **80%+** | Percentage of participants who complete at least 24 of 30 sessions. |
| **Tool Recall** | **75%+** | Percentage of participants who can name and describe at least one WRH tool by the final session. |
| **Tool Application** | **60%+** | Percentage of participants who report using at least one WRH tool, such as Pattern Veto or Plan B View, during the program. |
| **Baseline Recognition** | **80%+** | Percentage of participants who can identify their personal low-level dysregulation signal, referred to in WRH as the “Hum.” |
| **30-Day Follow-Up** | **60%+** | Percentage of reachable participants who still remember or report using at least one WRH concept 30 days after the program ends. |

---

## **Implementation Indicators**
In addition to participant metrics, the pilot should track whether the curriculum is usable for the host site and facilitators.

| Indicator | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Facilitator Usability** | Whether session guides are clear enough for consistent delivery. |
| **Participant Load Tolerance** | Whether sessions can be completed without requiring personal trauma disclosure. |
| **Attendance Patterns** | Whether participants remain engaged across the 30-session sequence. |
| **Tool Comprehension** | Whether participants can explain concepts in their own words. |
| **Site Fit** | Whether the host organization can integrate WRH into its existing schedule, staffing, and safety procedures. |

## **Qualitative Feedback Plan**
Anonymized participant and facilitator feedback should be added only after the pilot produces real responses and the host site approves the use of de-identified language.

Suggested feedback categories:

- Participant comprehension
- Participant safety and comfort
- Tool recall
- Tool use between sessions
- Facilitator ease of delivery
- Group engagement
- Barriers to implementation
- Recommended revisions
