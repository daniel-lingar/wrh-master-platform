## Overview

The WRH — **What Really Happened** — Master Platform is a peer-led, non-clinical trauma-literacy and survival-pattern education ecosystem designed for high-friction populations and high-barrier institutional environments.

WRH is built for settings where traditional clinical language often fails to reach participants, where shame and defensiveness create dropout risk, and where facilitators need clear, repeatable language that works under pressure.

This repository serves as the single source of truth for WRH deployment architecture, including curriculum materials, facilitator standardization tools, pilot evaluation logic, and institutional implementation documentation.

WRH is **not therapy, diagnosis, medical treatment, crisis intervention, or clinical counseling**. It is a structured psychoeducation and pattern-recognition framework designed to help participants understand survival-mode behavior, executive-function disruption, freeze responses, shame loops, and automatic reaction patterns in plain language.

---

## The Dual-Layer Architecture

WRH uses a deliberate dual-layer delivery model.

### 1. Operational Layer — Procurement, Administration, and Review

The operational layer translates WRH into institutional language for administrators, procurement officers, evaluators, program directors, and auditors.

This layer supports alignment with categories such as:

- Trauma-informed education
- Executive-function support
- Cognitive-behavioral alignment
- De-escalation education
- Peer-support curriculum delivery
- Reentry and diversion support
- Participant engagement and retention
- Non-clinical stabilization education

This layer is designed to make WRH readable to institutions without weakening the direct participant-facing language.

### 2. Lived-Experience Layer — Participant Delivery

The participant-facing layer uses direct, plain-language mechanics instead of clinical jargon.

WRH does not lead with diagnostic labels. It teaches participants to recognize patterns such as:

- Freeze State
- Red Zone
- Blue Zone
- Survival Software
- Autopilot
- Pattern Tracking
- System Logic Map
- Shutdown
- Explosion Phase
- Stalled Engine

This language is intentional. It is designed to reduce shame, lower defensiveness, and help participants understand what is happening inside their body and behavior before they are asked to change it.

## Pilot Deployment Model

The WRH Master Platform may be deployed as a limited pilot package before broader institutional adoption.

A standard pilot may include:

- Selected WRH curriculum sessions
- Facilitator delivery scripts
- Participant handouts
- Worksheets
- Fidelity checklists
- Implementation guide
- Non-clinical boundary statement
- Pilot evaluation framework
- Administrative summary
- Optional facilitator orientation

The pilot structure is designed to give institutions a controlled way to evaluate WRH before licensing or scaling a broader curriculum deployment.
