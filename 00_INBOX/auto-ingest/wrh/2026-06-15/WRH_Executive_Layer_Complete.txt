# 1. WRH Master Document

What Really Happened™ (WRH) is a structured behavioral and trauma-literacy model designed to help individuals and systems understand how survival patterns form and how directional change occurs.

WRH shifts the unit of intervention from **behavior** to **trajectory**.

WRH is built on two core engines:

- **The Survival Map** — where the pattern came from
- **The Trajectory Engine** — how direction changes

WRH is not a workbook, not a coping-skills program, and not a clinical treatment. It is a system-level model for understanding and changing human patterns.

# 5. Identity

WRH is a structured behavioral and trauma-literacy model designed to help individuals and systems understand how survival patterns form and how directional change occurs.

WRH shifts the unit of intervention from **behavior** to **trajectory**.

WRH provides:

- a shared language
- a mechanical model
- a repeatable process
- a system-level understanding of patterns
- a directional framework for change

## What WRH Is Not

WRH is not:

- a clinical treatment
- a coping-skills curriculum
- a workbook
- a motivational program
- a crisis-response protocol

These tools may exist in the ecosystem, but they are not the identity of WRH.

## WRH as a System Architecture

WRH is a system-level architecture for understanding and changing human patterns. It gives agencies a shared language, a mechanical model, a repeatable process, a directional framework, and a way to track change over time.

WRH is the operating system, not the application.

## Foundational Engines

- **The Survival Map** — the trauma-literacy model
- **The Trajectory Engine** — the directional-change model

These engines form the foundation of the entire WRH ecosystem.

# 6. Survival Map

The Survival Map is WRH’s five-part trauma-literacy model.

It explains where the pattern came from before asking anyone to change the pattern.

Most systems begin with behavior. WRH begins with context.

The Survival Map gives participants, staff, agencies, and partners a shared language for understanding survival patterns without requiring disclosure, exposure, confession, or clinical processing.

It does not ask: “What is wrong with this person?”

It asks: “What did this person’s system learn to do in order to survive?”

## The Five Parts

### 1. The Water

The Water represents the conditions that shaped the nervous system. It includes environment, pressure, instability, threat, loss, neglect, conflict, fear, humiliation, abandonment, isolation, or repeated stress.

The Water is not an excuse. It is the context.

### 2. The Alarm

The Alarm is the nervous system’s danger signal. It explains why danger can feel present even when the current moment does not appear dangerous to others.

The Alarm may show up as shutdown, panic, anger, avoidance, dissociation, hypervigilance, control, urgency, emotional flooding, refusal, or disappearance.

### 3. Survival Moves

Survival Moves are automatic responses developed to get through danger, pressure, shame, instability, or emotional overwhelm.

They may include fight, flight, freeze, fawn, shutdown, avoidance, substance use, control, withdrawal, self-sabotage, overfunctioning, disappearing, people-pleasing, defiance, or numbness.

Survival Moves are not random. They are learned responses.

### 4. The Rescue Boat

The Rescue Boat is the coping strategy that kept the person afloat.

A Rescue Boat can be harmful and still have once served a survival function. Examples include substances, isolation, anger, avoidance, overwork, emotional numbness, control, toxic relationships, compulsive behavior, disappearing, or shutdown.

WRH does not shame the Rescue Boat. It names what the Rescue Boat did, then asks what can replace it.

### 5. The Way Back

The Way Back is the process of building direction through small, repeatable interrupts.

It is not instant transformation, motivation, or punishment. It is repeated evidence.

The Way Back teaches participants and systems to notice the Alarm, name the Survival Move, identify the Rescue Boat, find the fork, practice the interrupt, repeat the action, build evidence, and stabilize direction.

## Summary

The Survival Map shows where the pattern came from so the Trajectory Engine can show how direction changes.

# 7. Trajectory Engine

The Trajectory Engine is WRH’s directional-change model.

It explains how a person moves from automatic survival pattern to repeated, measurable change.

Most systems focus on behavior after it appears. WRH focuses on the sequence that produces the behavior.

The sequence is:

**Pattern → Fork → Interrupt → Repetition → Evidence → Direction**

## 1. Pattern

A pattern is the repeated survival sequence a person’s system has learned to run. It may look like relapse, shutdown, avoidance, anger, disappearing, missed appointments, conflict, self-sabotage, emotional flooding, overfunctioning, or collapse.

The behavior is the end of the sequence. The pattern began earlier. The first task is to make the pattern visible.

## 2. Fork

The fork is the moment where direction can change. It is the point between automatic activation and automatic action.

The fork may be small and may last only seconds, but it matters because this is where interruption becomes possible.

## 3. Interrupt

An interrupt is a small action that breaks the automatic sequence.

It is not total transformation, perfection, or proof that the person is fixed.

An interrupt may be pausing, naming the Alarm, leaving before escalation, asking for help, using a safer replacement, showing up late instead of disappearing, sending a message instead of shutting down, or choosing one stabilizing action before the pattern completes.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is evidence.

## 4. Repetition

One interrupt does not change a trajectory. Repetition does.

Repetition turns understanding into movement. Each repeated interrupt weakens the old automatic sequence and strengthens the new direction.

## 5. Evidence

Evidence is what the system collects when a new action is repeated. Evidence tells the nervous system that this did not destroy me, this did not make me unsafe, this did not collapse everything, this created a different outcome, and this direction may be possible again.

Evidence is lived proof.

## 6. Direction

Direction is the stabilized movement that forms after repeated interrupts build enough evidence.

Direction does not mean the person never struggles. It means the pattern is no longer the only available route.

Direction may look like increased retention, reduced crisis use, fewer relapses, fewer missed appointments, improved communication, safer support use, increased stability, improved follow-through, greater staff confidence, and measurable participant movement.

## Summary

The Trajectory Engine shows how change happens: not through punishment, pressure, or willpower, but through repeated interrupts that build evidence and create direction.

# 8. Program System

The WRH Program System turns the WRH model into teachable, repeatable, agency-ready delivery.

If the Survival Map explains where the pattern came from, and the Trajectory Engine explains how direction changes, the Program System explains how to teach WRH, deliver WRH, and make WRH repeatable.

The Program System includes:

- Staff Training
- Participant Workshops
- Implementation Support
- Evaluation System

## Staff Training

Staff Training gives staff a shared language, mechanical model, way to understand behavior without blame, coaching framework, method for identifying forks and interrupts, and structure for supporting directional change.

Staff Training includes the Survival Map, the Trajectory Engine, Applied WRH, and Leadership & Fidelity.

## Participant Workshops

Participant Workshops translate the WRH model into direct service.

Workshops focus on pattern visibility, fork recognition, interrupt practice, safer replacements, evidence building, and directional stability.

They are non-clinical, non-exposure-based, non-confessional, and non-punitive.

## Implementation Support

Implementation Support ensures WRH is delivered consistently across staff, shifts, and programs.

It includes coaching, fidelity monitoring, case consultation, staff support, workflow integration, and alignment with agency goals.

Without implementation support, WRH becomes a training. With it, WRH becomes a program.

## Evaluation System

The Evaluation System measures interrupt frequency, evidence accumulation, directional stability, crisis utilization, retention, relapse/recidivism, and participant movement.

It turns WRH from a good idea into a measurable program.

## Summary

The WRH Program System turns the WRH model into a repeatable, measurable, agency-ready program that staff can deliver and participants can use.

- Product Catalog
- Training Catalog
- Pilot Program Sheet
- Pricing Sheet
- Statement of Work
- License Terms
- Evaluation Framework
- Reporting Tools
- Session 16 Sample Packet

## Component Purposes

The One-Page Sell Sheet gives directors and decision-makers a fast overview of WRH.

The Product Catalog lists all WRH offerings, including staff training, participant workshops, implementation support, evaluation tools, licensing options, and deployment packages.

The Training Catalog details staff-facing modules, learning objectives, delivery format, duration, audience, and prerequisites.

The Pilot Program Sheet outlines the 90-day WRH pilot.

The Pricing Sheet provides transparent pricing, tiered options, per-site or per-program models, licensing structure, and add-ons.

The Evaluation Framework explains what WRH measures and how outcomes are reported.

Reporting Tools include monthly reports, quarterly summaries, annual outcomes, fidelity snapshots, and participant movement indicators.

The Session 16 Sample Packet is proof of product.

## Summary

The WRH Buyer-Facing System gives agencies a clear, structured understanding of what WRH is, what they can purchase, and how the model delivers measurable outcomes.

# 10. Federal System

The WRH Federal System translates WRH into public-sector, grant, agency, and federal-review language.

It explains how WRH fits into funding environments, agency priorities, evaluation expectations, and compliance-facing systems.

This module is not the curriculum, buyer packet, or deployment plan. It is the federal-facing architecture of WRH.

The Federal System includes:

- Federal Abstract
- Needs Statement
- Project Description
- Goals & Objectives
- Logic Model
- Work Plan / Timeline
- Budget Justification Narrative
- Performance Measurement Plan
- Evaluation Plan
- Organizational Capacity Statement
- Sustainability Plan
- SAMHSA Compliance Language
- Letters of Support Templates

## Component Purposes

The Federal Abstract summarizes what WRH is, who it serves, why the project matters, expected outcomes, and public-sector alignment.

The Needs Statement explains the structural gap WRH addresses: most systems intervene at the visible behavior stage after the survival sequence has already completed.

The Project Description explains how WRH works inside an agency through staff training, participant workshops, implementation support, evaluation tools, reporting, and sustainability planning.

Goals and Objectives translate WRH into measurable commitments.

The Logic Model connects inputs, activities, outputs, short-term outcomes, intermediate outcomes, and long-term outcomes.

The Work Plan explains implementation steps across 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, or 12 months.

The Budget Justification Narrative explains why each cost is necessary and connects every cost to a project function.

The Performance Measurement Plan tracks participant attendance, staff training completion, interrupt frequency, pattern visibility, evidence accumulation, directional stability, crisis utilization, retention, relapse/recidivism indicators, staff confidence, and fidelity markers.

The Evaluation Plan reviews delivery, staff use, participant understanding, interrupt attempts, stability indicators, crisis use, and usable agency data.

The Organizational Capacity Statement explains why the applicant or partner can execute WRH.

The Sustainability Plan explains how WRH continues after initial funding through internal staff training, train-the-trainer pathways, licensing, budget integration, partner support, annual updates, recurring cycles, outcome reporting, and future grant alignment.

SAMHSA-facing language remains clear, non-clinical, non-diagnostic, trauma-informed, and focused on education, prevention, early intervention, resilience, recovery support, workforce capacity, measurement, sustainability, and accessibility.

Letters of Support document partner alignment.

## Summary

The WRH Federal System translates WRH into grant-ready, agency-ready, and review-ready language so public systems can understand, fund, measure, and sustain the model.

It includes:

- Implementation Roadmap
- Fidelity Tools
- Participant Materials

## Deployment Checklist

The Deployment Checklist includes staff training schedule, workshop schedule, coaching cadence, reporting setup, fidelity expectations, evaluation alignment, participant flow, and documentation requirements.

## Implementation Roadmap

The Implementation Roadmap provides a month-by-month rollout plan covering training, workshops, coaching, fidelity checks, evaluation milestones, and reporting deadlines.

## Fidelity Tools

Fidelity Tools ensure WRH is delivered the way it was designed. They include facilitator checklists, session rubrics, coaching notes, observation tools, fidelity snapshots, and alignment guides.

These materials reinforce the Survival Map, Trajectory Engine, interrupt practice, evidence building, and directional stability.

They are non-clinical, non-exposure-based, non-confessional, and non-punitive.

## Summary

The WRH Deployment System ensures WRH is delivered consistently, predictably, and with fidelity so agencies can achieve measurable, stable, repeatable outcomes.

# 12. Sales System

The WRH Sales System is the structured communication layer that supports agencies, partners, and procurement officers as they move through the WRH decision process.

It is not marketing, persuasion, or pressure.

It is a professional, predictable, high-clarity communication system that helps agencies understand what WRH is, what WRH delivers, how WRH works, what they can purchase, how implementation unfolds, and how outcomes are measured.

The Sales System includes:

- Attachment Sequence
- Buyer Journey
- Follow-Up Scripts

## Attachment Sequence

The Attachment Sequence is the structured order in which WRH materials are sent to a buyer.

A typical sequence includes WRH One-Page Overview, WRH Master Document, Product Catalog, Pilot Program Sheet, Pricing Sheet, Statement of Work, Evaluation Framework, and Session 16 Sample Packet.

## Buyer Journey

The Buyer Journey maps how agencies move from first contact to contract.

It includes initial interest, informational meeting, document review, internal discussion, procurement review, scope alignment, pricing confirmation, contract drafting, and implementation scheduling.

Agencies may move back and forth between stages.

## Follow-Up Scripts

Follow-Up Scripts are professional communication templates that ensure clarity, consistency, accuracy, alignment, and tone discipline.

They include post-meeting summaries, document delivery messages, clarification responses, procurement guidance, next-step confirmations, timeline alignment messages, and evaluation explanation messages.

They help agencies feel supported, not pressured.

## Summary

The WRH Sales System provides a structured, professional communication pathway that helps agencies understand WRH, evaluate WRH, and move confidently toward implementation.

# 13. Brand System

The WRH Brand System defines the identity, tone, structure, and visual logic of the WRH ecosystem.

It ensures every WRH document — curriculum, executive materials, deployment tools, federal content, sales attachments, and training — feels like it comes from the same system.

The Brand System includes:

- Color Architecture
- Voice & Tone
- Structural Rules
- Document Identity
- Design Logic

This is not marketing. This is system identity.

## Color Architecture

The WRH Dark Mode Executive palette is built around five colors:

- Black — foundation
- Charcoal — structure
- Gold — authority
- White — clarity
- Purple — depth

Black anchors the system. Charcoal organizes content. Gold signals authority and key concepts. White provides readability and contrast. Purple marks depth, insight, and emotional intelligence.

## Voice & Tone

WRH voice is direct, grounded, mechanical, emotionally intelligent, high-authority, and non-performative.

WRH avoids motivational language, clinical jargon, emotional pressure, performative empathy, and inspirational slogans.

## Structural Rules

WRH documents use clear section headers, short paragraphs, mechanical explanations, no filler language, no emotional overreach, no unnecessary narrative, consistent hierarchy, and consistent summary lines.

This makes WRH readable, teachable, reviewable, fundable, and evaluable.

## Document Identity

- Executive Documents — system architecture
- Program Documents — training and workshops
- Deployment Documents — fidelity and implementation
- Federal Documents — compliance and alignment
- Sales Documents — procurement clarity
- Curriculum Documents — participant learning

Each category looks distinct, reads distinct, functions distinct, and still belongs to the same ecosystem.

## Design Logic

WRH design logic is built around clarity, hierarchy, contrast, authority, and minimalism.

Design is not decoration. Design is function.

## Summary

The WRH Brand System creates a unified, authoritative identity that makes WRH recognizable, trustworthy, and coherent across every layer of the ecosystem.
